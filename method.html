The Method

The Hive Method

A six-month cohort-based Agile culture reset for mid-size financial services and technology firms.

Why most programmes fail

External consultants bring frameworks. Frameworks don't change behaviour — people do. When the consultant leaves, the framework sits in a shared drive and the team reverts to what feels safe. The knowledge was never transferred. The ownership was never built.

Most transformation programmes are designed around delivery milestones, not cultural ones. They measure workshops run, not decisions made differently. They exit when the contract ends, not when the change is self-sustaining.

What I do differently

I don't train your teams on Agile. I build Agile Champions inside your organisation — people who carry the methodology, model the behaviours, and hold the standard after I'm gone. The programme runs for six months, in cohort format, across your engineering and delivery teams simultaneously. It's not a course. It's a cultural installation.

The design principle comes from an unlikely place: the hive. A healthy colony doesn't have a manager. It has shared purpose, clear roles, and the kind of distributed intelligence that no hierarchy can manufacture. The goal isn't to make your teams dependent on a framework — it's to build the conditions where the right behaviours emerge and sustain themselves.

Phase 1 — Diagnose (Months 1–2)

We diagnose not your process, but your culture. Where decisions stall. Where psychological safety is absent. Where the real bottlenecks live, beneath the ones on the roadmap. This phase involves structured interviews, team observations, and a delivery health assessment. The output is a clear picture of what's actually happening — which is rarely what the organisational chart suggests.

Phase 2 — Build (Months 3–4)

Selected leaders go through an intensive cohort programme, learning to facilitate change, surface tension constructively, and sustain Agile practice from within. This isn't a certification course. Participants are chosen because their peers already trust them — the programme gives them a language, a methodology, and the confidence to lead without formal authority.

Phase 3 — Transfer (Months 5–6)

The Champions lead. I step back. This is the phase most programmes skip entirely, which is exactly why most programmes fail. The final two months are designed to stress-test the transfer — to surface the moments where the old gravity pulls, and to ensure the Champions have the tools to hold the line. When this phase is complete, the programme is done. I don't build in renewal by design.

Who it's for

Mid-size firms in financial services and technology, typically 200 to 2,000 people. Headquartered or operating in Romania, CEE, or DACH. You've been through at least one transformation attempt. It didn't hold. You're ready to approach this differently.

My ideal client is a COO, CEO, or Head of Transformation who is technically fluent enough to know that the problem isn't the tooling, and frustrated enough to stop adding new frameworks on top of old ones.

Pricing and delivery

Engagements are priced between €85,000 and €120,000 depending on organisational size and scope. Delivered remotely, with optional on-site intensives at key cohort milestones. I work with a small number of organisations at any given time — typically two to three concurrent engagements — to ensure the work stays specific, not templated.
