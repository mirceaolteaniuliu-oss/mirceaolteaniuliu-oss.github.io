About

Mircea Oltean

Senior engineering and programme management leader. Transformation specialist. Former developer.

Where this started

Mircea spent the first decade of his career as a software developer and architect. He understands engineering culture from the inside — the way skilled technical people respond to top-down mandates, the reason standups become theatre, the particular frustration of a developer who knows a better way and has no channel to say so.

That background shapes everything about how he works. He doesn't arrive with a framework and a slide deck. He arrives with questions — the kind that take a few weeks to answer honestly, and the kind of answers that tend to make leadership uncomfortable before they make delivery faster.

The work

Over 15 years, Mircea has led transformation programmes across financial services and enterprise technology organisations in Romania, the UK, and across Central and Eastern Europe. He holds an MBA and a range of senior Agile and delivery certifications. He is not affiliated with any consultancy. He works with a small number of clients at any given time — which is a deliberate choice, not a capacity constraint.

His specialisation is the specific, expensive problem of organisations that are technically capable but culturally stuck — teams that can't sustain change after the consultant leaves. He has solved this problem at scale, with proof.

On beekeeping

Mircea keeps bees. Not as a hobby, but as a practice. The hive is a model he returns to often — not because it's a clever metaphor, but because it's a genuinely accurate one. A colony doesn't have a manager. It has shared purpose, clear roles, and the kind of distributed intelligence that no hierarchy can manufacture. Every individual knows what they're for. The whole is far more resilient than any single part.

Building that inside a company is harder than it sounds. It requires patience, an understanding of what conditions produce the right behaviour, and the discipline to stop intervening before the system has had time to stabilise. It also requires accepting that the best outcome is one where you are no longer needed.

That's the philosophy behind The Hive Method. And it's the reason the programme is designed to end.
